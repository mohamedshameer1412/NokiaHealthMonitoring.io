Project Abstract: Financial Health Monitoring for Nokia Corporation

In today’s fast-paced and competitive business environment, financial resilience and adaptability are crucial for any organization’s survival and growth...

The study delves into Nokia’s financial history...

  • Key financial patterns and vulnerabilities
  • Strategic missteps that influenced its financial health over time

Utilizing Python-based tools and machine learning techniques such as:

  • ARIMA for revenue forecasting
  • Linear Regression for profit margin estimation

The project demonstrates how predictive analytics can aid in proactive financial decision-making...

The success objectives include:

  • Evaluating Nokia’s liquidity, solvency, profitability, and operational efficiency
  • Identifying financial risks and long-term trends
  • Benchmarking Nokia’s performance against industry standards
  • Providing actionable recommendations for financial sustainability

Nokia Timeline (2009–2024)

2009
Revenue: $57.16B | Gross Profit: $18.50B

Peak revenue; dominant in mobile phones.

2010
Revenue: $56.36B | Gross Profit: $17.02B

Beginning of decline due to smartphone competition.

2011
Revenue: $53.84B | Gross Profit: $15.77B

Challenges from iPhone and Android continue.

2012
Revenue: $38.81B | Gross Profit: $10.79B

Significant loss; Lumia phones underperform.

2013
Revenue: $16.88B | Gross Profit: $7.10B

Sold handset division to Microsoft; pivot to networks.

2014
Revenue: $16.93B | Gross Profit: $7.50B

Stabilization post-handset sale.

2015
Revenue: $13.88B | Gross Profit: $6.06B

Revenue decline; networks are success business.

2016
Revenue: $26.13B | Gross Profit: $9.36B

Acquisition of Alcatel-Lucent boosts revenue.

2017
Revenue: $26.16B | Gross Profit: $10.33B

Steady performance in network infrastructure.

2018
Revenue: $26.65B | Gross Profit: $9.98B

Slight growth; challenges in 5G rollout.

2019
Revenue: $26.11B | Gross Profit: $9.33B

Flat revenue; increased competition in telecom.

2020
Revenue: $24.96B | Gross Profit: $9.36B

Impact of global economic slowdown.

2021
Revenue: $26.27B | Gross Profit: $10.45B

Recovery with increased 5G demand.

2022
Revenue: $26.25B | Gross Profit: $10.77B

Continued growth; strong 5G infrastructure sales.

2023
Revenue: $24.09B | Gross Profit: $9.40B

Decline due to reduced telecom investments.

2024
Revenue: $20.80B | Gross Profit: $9.59B

Further revenue drop; cost-cutting measures implemented.

Summary
  • 2009–2011: Peak performance, followed by decline due to smartphone competition.
  • 2012–2013: Major loss and sale of handset division.
  • 2016: Acquisition of Alcatel-Lucent lifts revenue.
  • 2021–2022: Boost from 5G demand.
  • 2023–2024: Revenue dip from economic strain and lower telecom investments.
